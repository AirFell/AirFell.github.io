Guns VS Games History

GvG was originally a re-branding of my oldschool YouTube channel comprised of gameplay from Battlefield Bad Company 2 with Exiles Clan, Mortal Online gameplay with Forsaken Guild, and other personal things such as my old band Poor Bastards in Jail and other artistic attempts.


After re-discovering firearm culture as an adult I wanted a way to merge it with my love of gaming- specifically FPS gaming.

Seeing youtube as an outlet for my urges to be creative and potentially make an imcome, I went out to buy, borrow or build firearms to compare them to the ones I regularly see in video games. It turns out guns are expensive.

Shortly after beginning a good schedule of Tuesday uploads my channel was demonitized by YouTube for not providing viewer friendly content. At that point I moved on to just streaming on YouTube, then Twitch.

In an attempt to revive my demonitized channel I have removed ALL ads from my channel and use 100% copyright free material, either outsourcing from other artists with their concent via Soundcloud and other open source music sites, or when I have time creating my own musical content.

Due to this, I ask for you- the viewer to use the contact button and drop some suggestions as well as check out the Goals tab on this site and consider donating towards future episodes of Guns VS Games.

Thank you for your support!

AirFell's History

You could say my gaming history started in Doom, possibly Quake, or even Unreal Tournament (original) for online play. I don't think that would be fair.


We all know it started with N64 and 007 Goldeneye, then Perfect Dark. I'm on the edge of Gen X and Millenial (shit I hate that term). We all grew up on Goldeneye and those wonky N64 controllers. I'm a lefty, it became a boost, not a boon to my performance against my friends.


I was good, typically on the top of the boards, but it became a matter of repitition and psychology. AI at the time in any shooter was worse. Online play brought out a whole new world of competition and fun, which led to Unreal over dial up. Still not ideal.


Fast forward quite some time for being a teen and dreams of being a rockstar, I came back to PC on Battlefield 2 after discovering The Exiles clan. Over time that morphed into Project Reality addiction and milsims. I must be a masochist, I thrive on difficulty.

Taking a break I moved onto something new, MMORPG's with Shadowbane, then later Mortal Online, because if its not full loot and difficult, whats the point?

As time has gone by I've jumped from game to game, but one thing stays true, it must be difficult. We're in a golden age of FPS' right now. ARMA 3 and SQUAD for milsim, PUBG, DayZ, Escape from Tarkov... the list goes on and on.

Why can't medieval and fantasy MMORPG's jump on board? My hopes are in Life is Feudal.


Catch me on Twitch.tv/Airfell if you wanna hang out!
